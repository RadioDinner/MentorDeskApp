MENTORDESK GLOSSARY
====================

This document defines the terminology used across the MentorDesk project.
All contributors and sessions should reference this glossary for consistency.


PLATFORM & PRODUCT TERMS
--------------------------

App
    The primary web application used by coaching organizations, individual
    mentors, or coaches to manage their mentoring business. This is the
    core product -- analogous to the Microsoft 365 admin portal. It is
    where organizations add mentors, add mentees, build courses, bill
    mentees, and track staff payments. When used colloquially, "app"
    refers to the web app. The term also encompasses the iOS and Android
    apps collectively when speaking generally about the product.

Web App
    The browser-based version of the App. This is the primary interface
    and the first platform being built.

iOS App
    The native Apple (iPhone/iPad) version of the App. Refers explicitly
    and exclusively to the iOS build.

Android App
    The native Android version of the App. Refers explicitly and
    exclusively to the Android build.

MentorDesk
    The name of the overall product/platform. MentorDesk is the software
    that coaching organizations license and use.


USER ROLES
-----------

Organization (Org)
    A coaching company, mentoring firm, or training organization that
    licenses and uses MentorDesk. An organization is a tenant in the
    multi-tenant system.

Admin
    An organization-level administrator who has full access to manage
    mentors, mentees, staff, offerings, billing, and settings within
    their organization.

Mentor / Coach
    An individual who delivers mentoring, coaching, or training sessions
    to mentees. Can exist within an organization or operate independently
    as a solo practitioner.

Mentee / Trainee / Client
    An individual receiving mentoring, coaching, or training. Enrolled
    in offerings and billed through the platform.

Staff / Org Staff
    The people inside a coaching organization (the organization that
    licenses MentorDesk) who use the platform to run their business.
    This includes admins, mentors, and operational staff members.
    "Staff" and "org staff" refer exclusively to personnel within the
    client organization -- NOT Anthropic, not MentorDesk platform
    employees, and not the development team. Staff roles:
      - Admin: full access to manage the org's MentorDesk instance
      - Mentor/Coach: delivers sessions to mentees
      - Staff: general operational staff tracked for payroll purposes


BUSINESS CONCEPTS
------------------

Offering
    A product or service provided by an organization. Offerings come in
    two types: Courses and Engagements.

Course
    A type of offering. A structured content program -- may include
    lessons, modules, videos, and assessments. Can be one-time or
    recurring.

Engagement
    A type of offering. A meeting-based mentoring program, typically
    involving scheduled sessions between a mentor and mentee. May be
    billed on a recurring basis.

Assignment
    A pairing of a mentor to a mentee within an organization. Assignments
    track the relationship status (active, paused, ended) and are managed
    by admins through the Assignments module.

Meeting
    A scheduled session between a mentor and mentee, typically part of
    an engagement. Credits are consumed when meetings are booked.

Credit
    A unit representing a mentee's entitlement to schedule meetings
    within an arrangement. Credits are granted per billing period and
    may or may not roll over.

Invoice
    A billing record for a mentee, tracking amounts owed and payment
    status for enrolled offerings.


UI CONCEPTS
------------

Module
    A top-level navigation item in the left sidebar of the app. Modules
    are organized into groups (e.g. Main, People, Business, Finance,
    System). Each module corresponds to a page or feature area in the
    application.

Module Group
    A labeled section in the sidebar that contains related modules.
    Current groups: Main, People, Business, Finance, System.


BRANDING & CUSTOMIZATION
-------------------------

Org Branding
    The customization layer available to each organization. Currently
    includes logo and color theme. MentorDesk remains the visible
    product name (Shopify-style branding, not full white-label).

Theme
    The set of brand colors an organization configures to customize
    the look of their MentorDesk instance.


TEST & DEVELOPMENT
-------------------

TechBeans
    The test organization used during development. All seed data and
    initial testing is done under this org.


TECHNICAL TERMS
----------------

Tenant
    A single organization's isolated data space within the multi-tenant
    system. Each tenant has its own mentors, mentees, offerings, and
    settings.

Multi-tenancy
    The architecture pattern where a single deployment of MentorDesk
    serves multiple organizations, with data isolation between them.

Supabase
    The backend platform used for database (PostgreSQL), authentication,
    storage, and serverless functions.

RLS (Row-Level Security)
    PostgreSQL feature used to enforce tenant isolation -- ensures users
    can only access data belonging to their organization.
